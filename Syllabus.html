Course Overview Description & materials

Course Description
Covers accounting procedures as they apply to the management function of decision making, including cost definitions, cost tracking, variance analysis, budgeting, and quantitative methods for managerial decisions. Prerequisite: C or better in ACCT 131. (PCS 1.1, 3 credit hours: 3 hours lecture, 0 hours lab).
Required Materials
Financial and Managerial Accounting, Wild, McGraw Hill, latest edition.

Learning Objectives Upon successful completion

  1. Analyze and classify costs (fixed, variable, direct, indirect, product, and period costs).
  2. Apply job‑order and process costing to support management decisions.
  3. Calculate and interpret variances from standard costs.
  4. Construct and analyze cost‑volume‑profit relationships using absorption and variable costing.
  5. Apply activity‑based costing and compare to traditional methods.
  6. Prepare and interpret master budgets and related financial statements.
  7. Evaluate ethical implications of managerial accounting decisions.
  8. Prepare and analyze financial statements, including the statement of cash flows.
  9. Perform profitability and performance analysis using ratios and scorecards.
  10. Demonstrate professional communication and teamwork.
  11. Apply present value methods and differential cost analysis in decision making.
