Catalog Description

This course covers managerial accounting concepts that support internal decision making, including cost classification, cost accumulation systems, standard costing and variance analysis, budgeting, performance measurement, and capital budgeting tools. [file:2]

Emphasis is placed on using accounting information for planning, controlling operations, evaluating performance, and making short‑ and long‑term business decisions. [file:2]

Course Learning Outcomes
  • Classify and analyze costs as fixed, variable, direct, indirect, product, and period costs. [file:2]
  • Complete job‑order and process costing cycles with appropriate journal entries and cost flow reports. [file:2]
  • Compute and interpret standard cost variances for materials, labor, and overhead. [file:2]
  • Use cost‑volume‑profit relationships and variable vs. absorption costing for decision making. [file:2]
  • Apply activity‑based costing to manufacturing and service organizations. [file:2]
  • Prepare master budgets, cash budgets, and pro‑forma financial statements. [file:2]
  • Evaluate performance using financial ratios, ROI, residual income, and balanced scorecard measures. [file:2]
  • Analyze relevant costs for special orders, make‑or‑buy, and other short‑term decisions. [file:2]
  • Evaluate capital investments using payback, accounting rate of return, NPV, and IRR. [file:2]
  • Demonstrate ethical reasoning in managerial accounting decisions and communicate findings professionally. [file:2]
